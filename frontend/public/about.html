How to use the dashboard

This workspace lets you replay a Grand Prix with a what-if pit strategy and see how the gap, positions, and end-of-race time might change—side by side with the real race trace.

Use it when you want to ask: “If this driver had stopped earlier or later, would they have been closer to the leader at the end?” or “Did that extra stop cost time overall?”

Workflow

How to use the dashboard

  1. Pick a race and the driver you want to experiment with (from the controls at the top).
  2. In the stint bar, drag that driver’s pit markers to move stops to new laps—this is your edited strategy.
  3. Run Simulate after editing, then use Show Simulated to overlay the simulator run on the charts.
  4. Compare the race trace, pit window heatmap, and delta breakdown to the baseline run: you’re looking for a better or worse gap at the flag and clearer timing trade-offs.
  5. Read gain/loss in the pit window and the signed seconds in the delta breakdown as relative improvements or costs—not as exact broadcast timing.

Simulation

What the simulator considers

When you move a pit stop, the app rebuilds a virtual stint plan—which compound you’re on each lap and how old the tyres are—then rolls the race forward by adjusting lap times vs the real race. Here’s what feeds that:

  • Tyre compound and tyre age — After your edit, each lap knows the simulated compound and how many laps that set has done. That drives how pace differs from what actually happened on the same lap.
  • Pit-stop time cost — Extra time lost on in-lap / stop / out-lap is estimated from the race data (with sensible bounds). Optional runs can add a bit of random spread around that.
  • Pace vs rubber state — Small adjustments compare simulated vs actual tyre age and compound (for example fresher softs vs older mediums). When enabled, a pace helper trained on similar laps can refine that delta.
  • Traffic and rejoin — Uses gaps to cars ahead and behind from the trace so tight packs can add a short rejoin penalty right after a stop.
  • Random variation — If the backend runs multiple Monte Carlo samples, results summarize the spread (median and band)—useful to see uncertainty, not a single magic number.

Not modeled as full physics: weather changes, safety cars, and blue flags are not replayed—only the strategy swap and the factors above shape the what-if curve.

Chart guide

Race trace

Shows each selected driver’s gap to the race leader on every lap.

Use it to

  • See whether your strategy helps a driver close on the leader or drop back after a stop.
  • Overlay simulated vs actual when simulation is on, and compare the line at the end of the race.

Look for

  • Sharp steps at pit laps—undercuts and out-laps often show as a jump or dip in gap.
  • Recovery on fresh tyres: the line flattening or trending back toward the leader.
  • Whether the simulated trace finishes higher or lower than before (closer or farther from the leader).

Position flow

Shows race position (P1, P2, …) for each driver over laps.

Use it to

  • Follow who passed whom and when position changes line up with pit stops or battles.
  • Connect strategy changes in the stint bar to real field position—not just gap to the leader.

Look for

  • Lines crossing when positions swap; big vertical moves after stops or safety-car bunching.
  • Whether your driver’s line ends higher or lower in the order when you simulate a new strategy.

Stint bar / stint history

Shows each driver’s tyre compound and stint length in laps, end to end.

Use it to

  • Edit strategy: drag pit stop handles to change which lap a stop happens on.
  • See at a glance how many stints and which compounds your what-if plan uses compared to the original race.

Look for

  • The highlighted / modified row when you’ve changed that driver’s stops.
  • Longer or shorter colour blocks when you move a stop—those blocks are the stints the simulator will run.

Pit window heatmap

Shows estimated time gained or lost from pitting in a window of laps around each candidate stop, relative to a baseline.

Use it to

  • Spot favourable windows (greener) where an extra stop or earlier stop might pay off.
  • Compare before and after you change the stint plan—cells update with the simulation.

Look for

  • Clusters of strong positive or negative values along the lap axis—that’s where timing sensitivity is highest.
  • It does not show race order; it’s a timing map only.

Delta breakdown

With simulation on, splits the driver’s gap vs actual at the finish into parts—pit timing, tyre/pace, traffic/rejoin, and net.

Use it to

  • See which factors pushed the simulated run toward or away from the real result at the flag.
  • Read the net bar as the overall signed gap vs actual; factor bars show how much each piece contributed.

Look for

  • Bars to the right of centre (positive) vs left (negative)—sign convention matches the axis label.
  • Whether net lines up with what you saw in the race trace at the last lap.

About the numbers: Charts update from the strategy simulator in this app. They are built for comparison between baseline and what-if runs and for timing insight—not for predicting exact live TV gaps or real-world lap times.
